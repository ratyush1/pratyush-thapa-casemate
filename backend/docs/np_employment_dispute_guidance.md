# Employment Dispute Guidance (Nepal)

Last reviewed: 2026-03-14
Jurisdiction: Nepal
Law: Labor Act 2074 (2017), Social Security Fund Act 2074, Bonus Act 2030

## Key rights under Nepal Labor Act 2074

Every employee in Nepal has the right to receive a written employment contract under Section 8 of the Labor Act 2074. The standard workweek is 8 hours per day and 48 hours per week. Overtime work must be paid at 150% of the regular hourly rate.

Employees are entitled to 18 days of annual leave per year after completing one year of service, and 12 days of paid sick leave per year. Female employees are entitled to 98 days of maternity leave (60 days with pay).

## Termination and notice period

An employer must provide 30 days written notice before terminating an employee without cause, or pay one month's salary in lieu of notice. Termination without proper notice or valid reason is considered unfair dismissal under Chapter 10 of the Labor Act 2074.

For retrenchment due to business reasons, the employer must pay severance of one month's full salary for each year of completed service.

Gratuity is calculated at 8.33% of basic salary per year of service and must be paid upon separation if the employee has served for at least one year. Final settlement of all dues including salary, leave encashment, and gratuity must be made within 30 days of separation.

## Unfair dismissal and salary disputes

If you believe you have been unfairly dismissed or your salary has not been paid, you must file a complaint at the nearest Department of Labour or Labor Office within 35 days of the disputed event. Failing to file within 35 days may result in loss of your right to claim. Keep copies of your employment letter, salary slips, and any written communications as evidence.

The Labor Office has authority to order reinstatement or payment of back wages and compensation. If unsatisfied with the Labor Office decision, you can appeal to the Labor Court within 35 days of the order.

## Workplace safety and harassment

Every employer is required to maintain a safe working environment under the Occupational Safety Act. Sexual harassment at the workplace is prohibited under the Labor Act 2074 and the Workplace Harassment Act. Victims can file a complaint with the employer's internal committee or directly with the Labor Office.

## Documents to gather

Gather the following before filing a complaint: written employment contract or appointment letter, salary slips for the relevant months, termination or suspension letter if any, bank transfer records of salary payments, emails or messages about the dispute, and witness names if available.

## Next steps if your rights are violated

If your salary is unpaid or delayed beyond 30 days, file immediately at the District Labor Office with your salary slips and employment letter. If you were terminated without notice or valid reason, file an unfair dismissal complaint at the Labor Office within 35 days. If you face verbal or written harassment at work, document all incidents with dates and file with the employer's grievance committee first, then the Labor Office if unresolved within 30 days.

## Safety note

This is general legal information and not legal advice. For your specific situation, consulting a licensed lawyer or visiting the nearest Nepal Bar Association legal aid desk is recommended.
