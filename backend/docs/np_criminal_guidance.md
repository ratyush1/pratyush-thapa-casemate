# Criminal Law Guidance (Nepal)

Last reviewed: 2026-03-14
Jurisdiction: Nepal
Law: Muluki Criminal Code 2074 (2017), Muluki Criminal Procedure Code 2074, Legal Aid Act 1997

## Understanding criminal complaints in Nepal

A criminal complaint in Nepal begins with filing a First Information Report (FIR), called कसूरसम्बन्धी जाहेरी in Nepali, at the nearest police station. The police are legally required to register the FIR if the complaint concerns a cognizable offense. If police refuse to register, you can file a complaint directly to the District Court, which can order police to investigate.

## Arrest and police custody rights

If you or someone you know has been arrested, the arrested person has the right to be informed of the reason for arrest immediately. The arrested person must be produced before a magistrate within 24 hours of arrest. The arrested person has the right to remain silent and the right to consult a lawyer before questioning. Police cannot hold a person beyond 24 hours without producing them before a magistrate. Bail can be applied for at the first appearance before the magistrate.

## Bail in Nepal

Bail is available for most offenses under the Muluki Criminal Procedure Code 2074. For bailable offenses, bail must be granted as a matter of right. For serious non-bailable offenses such as murder, rape, or drug trafficking, bail is at the discretion of the court based on the severity of the offense, likelihood of flight, and risk to witnesses. A lawyer should apply for bail at the District Court hearing. If bail is initially refused, the application can be renewed when circumstances change, such as after extended time in remand.

## Remand and investigation period

Police may seek a court-approved remand order to detain a suspect for investigation. The maximum remand period is 25 days for most serious offenses. The detained person must be brought to court for each remand extension. During remand, the detained person has the right to be visited by family and to consult a lawyer.

## Victim rights and private prosecution

A crime victim has the right to participate in the criminal proceedings. If the public prosecutor does not adequately pursue the case, a victim may apply to the court to take over prosecution as a private party. Victims can seek compensation as part of the criminal judgment under the Muluki Criminal Code 2074.

## Theft, fraud, and property crimes

Theft (चोरीसम्बन्धी कसूर) under Section 295 of the Muluki Criminal Code is punishable by imprisonment and/or a fine proportional to the value stolen. Fraud and cheating (ठगी) is separately penalized under Section 306. If someone has defrauded you, file an FIR at the police station with written evidence, transaction records, and witness details.

## Cyber crime and online offenses

Cybercrimes including online fraud, defamation, harassment, and unauthorized access to computer systems are covered under the National Penal Code and the Electronic Transaction Act 2063. File a complaint at the Cyber Bureau of Nepal Police or at the nearest police station.

## Domestic violence as a criminal offense

Domestic violence is a criminal offense under the Domestic Violence (Crime and Punishment) Act 2066. The police must respond immediately to domestic violence calls. The victim can seek a temporary protection order from police or a longer-term protection order from the District Court to stop the abuser from contacting or approaching them.

## Legal aid for criminal cases

If you cannot afford a lawyer, free legal aid is available through the District Legal Aid Committee in each district. You can also contact the District Bar Association for pro bono assistance. In serious criminal cases, the court will appoint a lawyer if the accused cannot afford one.

## Documents and evidence to preserve

Preserve the following: written communications (messages, emails), photographs or recordings of any incident, medical reports for injury cases, bank statements and receipts for fraud cases, names and contact details of witnesses, and any FIR copy or court documents you have already received.

## Next steps if facing criminal charges or reporting a crime

If you need to file a complaint: go to the nearest police station with written facts and evidence. If police refuse, approach the District Court. If you have been arrested or charged: immediately contact a lawyer, exercise your right to remain silent, and have a family member apply for bail at your first court appearance. If you are a victim: file an FIR, gather evidence immediately before it is lost, and ask the court about victim compensation as part of sentencing.

## Safety note

This is general legal information and not legal advice. For your specific situation, consulting a licensed lawyer or the nearest Nepal Bar Association legal aid desk is recommended.
